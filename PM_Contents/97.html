Replacing the Line Filter Element (20K)

Maintenance period: Every 4000 Hours

WARNING

•Be sure to turn OFF the power supply and confirm that the lubricant unit has completely stopped before performing this procedure.

•Do not remove the element while pressure remains inside the filter. Otherwise, the unit can be damaged or lubricant can spurt out, resulting in an injury or other accident.

•Wear safety gloves and safety glasses when performing this procedure.

Procedure (Refer to Figure 6.4)

1)Turn OFF the power supply.

2)Place the oil pan under the line filter.

3)Turn the filter element [3] by hand, and then pull it out in the downward direction.

•While draining the lubricant from the filter element [3] into the oil pan, remove the filter element.

4)Apply lubricant to the gasket [2] for the new filter element [3].

NOTICE

Be careful that foreign matter does not adhere to the seal face of the basket [2]. The adherence of foreign matter cause the lubricant to leak.

5)Insert the new filter element [3] straight into the threaded portion [1] of the head unit.

6)Screw in the new filter element [3] by hand until the head unit and gasket [2] make contact.

7)Screw in the new filter element [3] by a further 1/2 to 2/3 turn.

NOTICE

Do not over tighten using a wrench, hammer or other tool.

8)Turn ON the power.

9)Test-run the machine, and confirm that lubricant does not leak from where the new filter element [3] is mounted.

This completes replacement of the line filter element.
