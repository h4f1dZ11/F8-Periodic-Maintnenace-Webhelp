Replacing the O Ring for the Sleeve

Maintenance period: Every 2000 Hours

WARNING

•Turn ON the machine power supply, and confirm that all machine movements have stopped prior to performing the following procedures.

•Exercise extreme caution as entry into the machine is necessary in order to perform the following procedures.

Procedure (Refer to Figure 4.2)

1)Turn ON the power.

2)Return the spindle-clamped tool to the tool magazine.

3)Move the spindle to an axis position where it is easy to perform work.

4)Open the operator door.

5)Place the spindle in the unclamp status.

6)Remove the snap ring [5] using the snap ring pliers.

7)Remove the cap [4] from the sleeve [3].

8)Remove the O ring [2] and backup ring [1] that are attached to the sleeve [3].

9)Clean the O ring grooves, and apply a small amount of grease or oil.

10)Attach a new O ring [2] and backup ring [1] to the sleeve [3].

11)Press the cap [4] against the sleeve [5] to attach the snap rings [5].

12)Clamp a through-spindle coolant tool in the spindle.

13)Close the operator door.

14)Check that the through-spindle coolant is discharged properly.

15)Stop the discharge of through-spindle coolant.

16)Open the operator door.

17)Remove the through-spindle coolant tool from the spindle.

18)Check that there is no cutting fluid leaking into the spindle taper.

This completes replacement of the O rings inside the sleeve.
